Adding value or features

I am a better writer because of Basecamp's text documents. I wanted to improve them. I had cool ideas. Like auto saving, hints on sentence structure, and improving readability. But they were not improving users' capability of sharing, collaborating and saving notes, so I decided to stop.

It's better to know when you're adding value and when you're adding features. I want to share the lessons learned from this experience. View the full image below. Scroll down for insights into my process and lessons learned.

Photoshop mockup

Concept

Just looking at the image above your thinking, "What the hell man you just changed the background from yellow to white." That's true, but there is more. Basecamp promotes good writing skills. I wanted to help make it's users better writers too. Stealing thunder from the Hemingway app I wanted to implement hints on sentence structure and punctuation.

HTML mockup

Lessons Learned

  • Assumptions will kill you. I had an idea and brought it to life. In the end it wasn't better than the current version, but I wouldn't have know unless I tried.

  • Omit needless features. It is easy to get caught up on cool ideas. But if they don't add value than their adding clutter and confusion.
